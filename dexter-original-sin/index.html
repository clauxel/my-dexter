The Original Sin of Financial Analysis

The original sin of financial research isn't making bad calls. It's spending the bulk of your research time on data retrieval — a task that provides zero analytical value.

Every analyst knows the cycle:

  1. Open a company's investor relations page
  2. Download the quarterly PDF or navigate to EDGAR
  3. Copy revenue, COGS, and operating income into a spreadsheet
  4. Repeat for 4–8 quarters, then do the same for 2–3 competitors
  5. Only then can you start the actual analysis

Steps 1–4 are pure overhead. They require precision and consistency but produce no insight on their own. They exist because financial data isn't automatically structured and compared — it has to be manually retrieved and organized.
