Your AI Financial Research Laboratory

In Dexter's Laboratory, a boy genius built an elaborate hidden lab where he ran experiments that were too complex, too large, and too ambitious for the ordinary world around him. The lab made the impossible routine.

Dexter AI is the financial research equivalent: a place where complex market questions — the kind that would take a human analyst hours — are run as structured experiments with live data and validated results. The lab makes it routine.

Research Experiments You Can Run in the Lab

📊 Earnings Experiment

"Compare the last 6 quarters of revenue and gross margin for NVIDIA and AMD." Dexter retrieves quarterly income statements for both, calculates margins, and returns a trend comparison.

💰 Cash Flow Scan

"Find S&P 500 companies with FCF yield above 8%." Dexter screens live balance sheet and cash flow data, ranks by yield, and returns a structured list with summaries.

🔍 Risk Analysis

"What are the key financial risks for Tesla going into Q4?" Dexter researches debt levels, margin pressures, competitive dynamics, and returns a risk summary with data citations.

📉 Margin Deep-Dive

"Why did Alphabet's operating margin decline last quarter?" Dexter traces revenue segments, operating cost changes, and identifies specific drivers with live data.

About Dexter's Laboratory (Cartoon Network)

Dexter's Laboratory is the classic Cartoon Network animated series that ran from 1996 to 2003. Created by Genndy Tartakovsky, the show follows Dexter, a child prodigy with a hidden laboratory in his bedroom, and his sister Dee Dee who constantly disrupts his experiments.

The show was one of Cartoon Network's earliest original productions and helped define the network's creative identity. It spawned two seasons plus a revival season. Reruns are available on Cartoon Network, HBO Max/Max, and various streaming services.

Fun fact: the original Dexter's Laboratory was inspired by Tartakovsky's interest in science fiction and his admiration for the 1950s–60s adventure cartoons. The character's iconic accent was based on famous scientists Tartakovsky admired as a child.
